The term bellwether comes from the practice of placing a bell around a castrated ram (known as a wether) whose job it is to lead a flock of sheep. Using these sounds from the bell, the farmers would be able to hear the bell and note the location of the flock when it could not be seen.

In politics, a bellwether seat is one whose voting tracks the overall federal election. In Australia, there are several seats that rarely if ever move from one party to the other, known as safe seats. A swing seat on the other hand is one that changes hands frequently, usually from the lnp to the alp or visa-versa. Some seats have earned the title of "bellwether seats", those that are won by the same party that wins the federal election.

It can be interesting to watch these seats, as seats are typically called individually before an election is called. If a seat is truly a bellwether seat, then polling done on said seats can be an important metric for people to judge overall trends on voting behaviors. The graph above (and the one below) takes the election data from the 1943 election to the 2019 election and showcases the party that won the seat vs the party that won the election federally.

For Eden-Monaro, from 1972 to 2013, the seat had a 100% track record for following the federal election. For a swing seat (over the last 17 elections), this is fairly impressive. Unfortunately in 2016, the seat lost its bellwether status, as it's been occupied by Labour over the last two elections, whereas the federal election has gone to the Coalition.

Since 1983 however, the seat of Robertson has been 100% accurate (over the last 14 elections), following the federal election. As you can see in the graph below, the seat has tracked the federal election since it fell to Labor in 1983. As Eden-Monaro serves as a warning, however, this is not guaranteed to last, and just because it has accurately tracked the federal election previously, there is no guarantee that this trend will last!


Update : As of the 2022 election, Robertson retains its position as the longest running bellwether seat.
