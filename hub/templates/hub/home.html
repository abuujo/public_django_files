Professional History


(Current) Reliability Data Analyst (AOR) / Navantia Australia

My current role is working with the navy in the AORSPO, looking after the HMAS Supply. My roles vary, from FRACAS reporting to being responsible for several key submissions to the navy to developing and implementing many process improvements taking advantage of VBA and Microsoft Access for automation. My understanding of VBA has greatly improved since joining Navantia, as it is the only tool I can work with inside the Defence setting.

Data Engineer & Reporting Specialist / OPEC Systems

At OPEC Systems I started out as an intern working on their reporting system for financial data. I learned the Excel add-in known as Jet Reporting and quickly became a subject matter expert on it. Using Jet Reporting I developed several key reports used by senior management to monitor and track key business functions. Later down the line, after my internship had ended, I began implementing many process changes to the development of key dashboards and reports.

I transitioned the company away from Jet Reporting towards Power BI and implemented several functions that took advantage of Power Automate. Among many developments, I helped deploy a Power BI Dashboard that tracked PFAS removal data, which was generated by scheduled PDF data submissions emailed to the firm from several contracted testing labs. This system gave real-time updates of the company's PFAS removal process and was used at several key presentations.

Data Engineer & Unit Tester / Umano

I began working at Umano temporarily as an aid to help clean and process data. Throughout my time there I took on many roles including developing many unit tests to achieve 95% coverage, implementing KPI analytics frameworks into a Python-based data analysis engine, and contributing to UI and Code design. My time at Umano gave me a much deeper understanding and appreciation of Python and data.

Data Analyst / Andiron Group
During my time with Andiron Group, I learned to analyse data to identify and bring out interesting and useful data to the forefront. Using anonymized credit card transactions (millions of data points) in collaboration with Pocketbook I found unique insights pertaining to how Australians spend their money on Valentine's day, how banks generate value from fees (FX fees etc.) and many other interesting insights. This was done using advanced custom queries on big data sets from Amazon Warehousing. During my time I contributed to a new categorisation system and many data standardisation and visualisation techniques. The insights gleaned from this data were features from places like Business Insider to Chanel 9 News.
